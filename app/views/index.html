What we tested
  • Understand ITT trainee awareness of ECF and what they need to know to feel prepared
  • Assess trainee understanding of/reaction to ECF introductory email
  • Understand how ECTs expect to track progress, what the value of progress tracking is
  • Time-bound/staggered design and strand overview with “time-to-complete” estimate
  • Understand how ECTs expect to work with mentors on CIP materials (using module reflection boxes as a prompt)
  • Further explore initial impressions of the platform, how they might consume the materials and whether they will print them or not
Findings
  • The email was see as supportive and encouraging, but users were confused and perceived assessment and induction as the same process
  • Tracking continued to test well with suggestions that it should include a traffic light system
  • Users were unclear if they should use their personal email (incase they moved school) or the school email
  • Check box at the end of the session was missing an option if you didn’t understand the content or wanted more time
  • Quizzes are seen as a valuable self-learning tool
  • Users like to see upcoming material but some find a lot of scrolling overwhelming
  • Users missed the full CIP course as they could not find 'self-study' on the navigation bar and only saw the first terms content
  • Users are happy to work through content on screen as opposed to printing
