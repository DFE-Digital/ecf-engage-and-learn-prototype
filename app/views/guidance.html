Training and support for new teachers

The one-year newly qualified teacher (NQT) induction programme for teachers in England has been replaced by an improved 2-year scheme.

It means teachers will get the best possible start to their careers after completing their initial teacher training (ITT).

The Department for Education has worked with teachers, school leaders, academics and education experts to develop structured training for the programme called the early career framework (ECF)

A range of free online training materials are available for teachers, mentors and school staff who run induction programmes.

At the end of their induction teachers will have their performance assessed to ensure they are meeting the Teachers’ Standards.

Topics teachers will cover during their induction are closely aligned to the Teachers’ Standards. They include:

  • setting high expectations
  • helping pupils progress
  • demonstrating good subject and curriculum knowledge
  • planning and teaching well-structured lessons
  • adapting to the different needs of pupils
  • making accurate and productive use of assessments
  • managing behaviour in the classroom
  • developing professional skills such as building relationships and communicating effectively

How teachers will do their training

Teachers will generally cover one or 2 topics a term. The topics will be broken down into manageable online sessions that last between 30 and 90 minutes.

There will also be time for one to one sessions with mentors, as well as online group sessions, webinars and ‘buddy’ programmes with other new teachers.

In total, teachers and their mentors will need to spend 10% of their working week away from the classroom in the first year of induction and 5% in the second year.

The DfE will pay schools for any extra staff needed to cover for this loss of teaching time.

How teachers will do their training

Schools are entitled to DfE-funded training for their induction teachers and mentors.

Free online materials designed by education experts are also available.

Alternatively, schools can design and deliver their own induction programme based on the ECF.
